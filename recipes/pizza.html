Pizza Napoletana

It's the original Italian pizza from Naples, famous for its light, chewy crust, simple tomato sauce, and melty mozzarella.

Just a few fresh ingredients, baked hot and fast, and you’ll have that authentic Italian flavor right at home.

Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 250 grams of flour (type 00)
  • 160ml fresh water (room temperature)
  • 2 grams of fresh yeast or 1 gram of dry yeast
  • 6 grams of salt

For the topping:

  • One can of San Marzano tomatoes (150g)
  • 125g of fresh Mozzarella cheese (Fior di latte or Buffalo)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Instructions

  1. Make the dough:

    2. Dissolve yeast in water, mix with flour and salt. Knead until smooth and elastic.
    Cover and let rise 2 hours (or overnight in the fridge).

  2. Prepare the sauce:

    3. Crush tomatoes by hand or blend briefly. Season with pinch of salt and olive oil.

  3. Shape the dough:

    4. Divide into two balls, rest 30 minutes. Flatten gently into 25-30cm rounds.

  4. Assemble:

    5. Spread tomato sauce evenly, add mozzarella, basil and drizzle of olive oil.

  5. Bake:

    6. In a wood-fired oven at 450–480 °C for 90 seconds (or as hot as your oven allows — use a pizza stone at 250 °C for 7–8 min).
